Testing and Verification of Agentic AI Systems

Welcome to the ISEC Workshop 2026 on Testing and Verification of Agentic AI Systems, co-located with the Innovations in Software Engineering Conference (ISEC). This full-day workshop is organized under the umbrella of ACM India and will be held at IIIT Hyderabad on June 20, 2026.

The workshop brings together researchers, practitioners, industry professionals, and students to address the urgent need for rigorous testing methodologies for autonomous AI agents deployed in safety-critical and mission-critical systems.

Abstract

Autonomous AI agents are increasingly embedded in safety-critical and mission-critical software systems — from healthcare decision support and financial trading to industrial automation and intelligent assistants. Unlike conventional software, these agentic systems exhibit non-deterministic behavior, emergent properties, and complex environmental interactions, making traditional testing and verification approaches inadequate.

This full-day workshop addresses the urgent need for rigorous, systematic methodologies to test and verify agentic AI systems. The workshop brings academic and industry participants together to cover robustification and alignment strategies, adversarial learning and resilience, systematic test case generation for autonomous agents, performance metrics beyond accuracy and cost (including PAC-based certification and harmonic mean metrics), and practical tools and frameworks such as RAGAS.

Motivation

The rapid deployment of agentic AI systems has outpaced the development of adequate testing and verification methodologies. This gap creates significant risks:

Why Traditional Testing Fails for Agents

  • Non-determinism: Agent outputs vary across runs due to stochastic components, environment dynamics, and LLM sampling.
  • Emergent Behavior: Complex outcomes arise from agent-environment interactions that cannot be predicted from component-level tests alone.
  • Safety-Critical Autonomy: Agents make autonomous decisions with real-world consequences (financial, medical, physical).
  • Alignment Drift: Ensuring agent goals and behaviors remain aligned with human intent over time and across contexts.
  • Exponential State Spaces: Testing burden grows combinatorially with environment complexity, tool availability, and multi-step reasoning chains.

Why Now

  • LLM-based agents (AutoGPT, CrewAI, LangGraph) are entering production at scale.
  • Regulatory pressure is mounting (EU AI Act, NIST AI RMF).
  • The software engineering community needs proven frameworks before deployment outpaces governance.

Objectives

  1. Understand the verification landscape and current research frontiers in agentic AI system testing.
  2. Learn robustification and alignment techniques for dependable behavior under perturbation.
  3. Apply adversarial testing methods, defense mechanisms, and resilience measurement for autonomous agents.
  4. Design systematic test suites for meaningful coverage of agent behavior spaces.
  5. Implement advanced metrics beyond accuracy — PAC-based certification, harmonic mean metrics, and multi-dimensional performance measurement.
  6. Gain hands-on familiarity with tools like RAGAS, LangSmith, and Guardrails AI for agent evaluation.
  7. Build professional networks connecting academia and industry for sustained collaboration.
