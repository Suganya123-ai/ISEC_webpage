Welcome to SERI Industry Day Workshop 2026

Welcome to SERI (Software Engineering Research and Innovation) Industry Day Workshop 2026, co-hosted by Infosys and IIIT Hyderabad.

This full-day workshop bridges the gap between cutting-edge academic research and industry practice, bringing together researchers, practitioners, and industry leaders to collaborate on one of the most critical challenges in modern software engineering.

The workshop focuses on Testing and Verification of Agentic AI Systems — a pressing need as autonomous AI agents transition from research prototypes to production deployments in safety-critical and mission-critical applications.

Join us on Saturday, July 18, 2026 at IIIT Hyderabad for a day of keynote presentations, invited talks, and in-depth panel discussions featuring 2 keynote speakers, 5 invited speakers, and 10 industry and academic panelists.

Objectives

  1. Bridge Academia-Industry Gap: Foster collaboration between academic researchers and industry practitioners working on AI system verification.
  2. Share Best Practices: Learn production-grade testing methodologies and lessons learned from deploying agentic AI at enterprise scale.
  3. Explore Research Frontiers: Gain insight into the verification landscape and current research directions in agentic AI system testing.
  4. Understand Robustification Techniques: Learn robustification and alignment approaches for dependable behavior under perturbation and deployment drift.
  5. Discuss Adversarial Methods: Explore adversarial testing methods, defense mechanisms, and resilience measurement for autonomous agents.
  6. Learn Test Suite Design Principles: Understand systematic approaches to test suite design for meaningful coverage of agent behavior spaces in production environments.
  7. Explore Advanced Metrics: Learn about advanced metrics beyond accuracy — including PAC-based certification, harmonic mean metrics, and multi-dimensional performance measurement.
  8. Discover Tools and Frameworks: Gain awareness of tools like RAGAS, LangSmith, and Guardrails AI for agent evaluation through expert presentations.
  9. Build Professional Networks: Strengthen professional networks connecting academia, industry, and research communities for sustained collaboration.

Abstract

Autonomous AI agents are increasingly embedded in safety-critical and mission-critical software systems — from healthcare decision support and financial trading to industrial automation and intelligent assistants. Unlike conventional software, these agentic systems exhibit non-deterministic behavior, emergent properties, and complex environmental interactions, making traditional testing and verification approaches inadequate. The rapid transition from research labs to production deployments has created a critical gap in testing methodologies and best practices.

This full-day industry-academia workshop addresses the urgent need for rigorous, systematic methodologies to test and verify agentic AI systems at enterprise scale. The workshop brings academic researchers and industry practitioners together to share insights, discuss practical challenges, and collaborate on solutions covering:

  • Production-Grade Testing: Real-world deployment challenges and testing strategies from industry leaders like Infosys
  • Robustification and Alignment: Ensuring agent behavior remains aligned with human intent under distribution shift and drift
  • Adversarial Learning and Resilience: Building defense mechanisms and measuring resilience of agentic systems
  • Systematic Test Case Generation: Creating comprehensive test suites for autonomous agents
  • Advanced Performance Metrics: Moving beyond accuracy to include cost, latency, safety, and compliance metrics
  • Tools and Frameworks: Expert presentations on RAGAS, LangSmith, and Guardrails AI for enterprise deployment

Motivation

Why Traditional Testing Fails for Agents

  • Non-determinism: Agent outputs vary across runs due to stochastic components, environment dynamics, and LLM sampling — making regression testing unreliable.
  • Emergent Behavior: Complex outcomes arise from agent-environment interactions that cannot be predicted from component-level tests alone.
  • Safety-Critical Autonomy: Agents make autonomous decisions with real-world consequences, requiring rigorous verification before deployment.
  • Alignment Drift: Goals and behaviors may diverge from human intent over time as agents adapt to new environments and scenarios.
  • Exponential State Spaces: Testing burden grows combinatorially with environment complexity, making exhaustive testing infeasible.
  • Regulatory Compliance: Increasing regulations (EU AI Act, NIST AI RMF) require demonstrable verification and compliance evidence.

Why Now: Industry Urgency

  • Production Deployments at Scale: LLM-based agents (AutoGPT, CrewAI, LangGraph) are entering production systems at enterprise scale.
  • Regulatory Pressure: Regulatory frameworks are emerging globally, requiring organizations to demonstrate robust testing and verification practices.
  • Safety and Trust Concerns: High-profile AI failures and incidents underscore the critical need for rigorous verification before deployment.
  • Industry Leadership: Forward-thinking organizations like Infosys are investing in production-grade testing methodologies and sharing their experience.
  • Community Need: The software engineering community needs proven frameworks, tools, and best practices before deployment outpaces governance.
