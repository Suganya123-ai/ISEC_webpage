Testing and Verification of Agentic AI Systems

A full-day workshop focused on rigorous testing methodologies, robustification, alignment, and verification frameworks for autonomous AI agents in safety-critical and mission-critical systems.

Abstract

Autonomous AI agents are increasingly embedded in safety-critical and mission-critical software systems, from healthcare decision support and financial trading to industrial automation and intelligent assistants. Unlike conventional software, these agentic systems exhibit non-deterministic behavior, emergent properties, and complex environmental interactions, making traditional testing and verification approaches inadequate.

This full-day workshop addresses the urgent need for rigorous, systematic methodologies to test and verify agentic AI systems. The workshop brings academic and industry participants together to cover robustification and alignment strategies, adversarial learning and resilience, systematic test case generation for autonomous agents, performance metrics beyond accuracy and cost (including PAC-based certification and harmonic mean metrics), and practical tools and frameworks such as RAGAS.

Motivation

The Testing Crisis for Agentic AI

Rapid deployment of agentic AI systems has outpaced the development of adequate testing and verification methodologies. This gap introduces significant technical and operational risk.

Why Traditional Testing Fails for Agents

  • Non-determinism: outputs vary across runs due to stochastic components and sampling.
  • Emergent behavior: agent-environment interactions produce hard-to-predict outcomes.
  • Safety-critical autonomy: autonomous decisions can have financial, medical, or physical consequences.
  • Alignment drift: goals and behavior may diverge from human intent over time and contexts.
  • Exponential state spaces: environment and tool complexity drives combinatorial test burden.

Current Gaps and Why Now

  • No standard methodology for autonomous agent test-suite generation.
  • Evaluation often stops at accuracy, ignoring cost, latency, robustness, explainability.
  • Adversarial testing remains ad hoc and incomplete.
  • Limited integrated tooling across fuzzing, property-based, metamorphic, and red-team methods.
  • Research and industry practice are still weakly connected.
  • LLM agents are entering production at scale while regulations are tightening (EU AI Act, NIST AI RMF).

Workshop Objectives

  1. Understand the verification landscape and current research frontiers in agentic AI testing.
  2. Learn robustification and alignment techniques for behavior under perturbation.
  3. Apply adversarial testing methods and resilience measurement approaches.
  4. Design systematic test suites for meaningful behavior-space coverage.
  5. Implement advanced metrics beyond accuracy, including PAC-based and harmonic approaches.
  6. Use modern frameworks such as RAGAS, LangSmith, Guardrails AI, and related tools.
  7. Build networks across academia and industry for sustained collaboration.
